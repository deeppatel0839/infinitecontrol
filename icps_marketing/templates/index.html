Motor Control Centers

Centralized, multi-section MCC assemblies for industrial motor management.

What We Build

A Motor Control Center (MCC) is the most efficient way to manage multiple electric motors from a single, centralized enclosure. We fabricate custom MCC assemblies ranging from simple 2-section units to large multi-section lineups managing dozens of motors in a single row. Each bucket (motor starter unit) is custom-configured with the right combination of disconnect means, overload relay, contactor, and protective devices for the motor it serves.

Our MCC assemblies are wired with copper bus bars rated for your full-load current, with short-circuit current ratings (SCCR) properly calculated and labeled. We can integrate variable frequency drives, soft starters, and intelligent motor protection relays directly into the MCC structure, eliminating the need for separate VFD enclosures and reducing overall footprint.

Key Features & Capabilities

Multi-section lineups with horizontal and vertical bus bars
Full-voltage, reduced-voltage, and soft-start motor starters
Integrated VFD buckets (15HP – 200HP+) within MCC structure
Bimetallic and electronic overload relays for each motor
Fusible and non-fusible disconnect switches per circuit
Phase loss, phase reversal, and under-voltage protection
Copper bus rated 600A – 2,000A main bus
Deadfront and drawout construction options available

VFD Drive Panels

Precision variable frequency drive enclosures for energy-efficient motor control.

What We Build

Variable Frequency Drive (VFD) panels give you precise control over motor speed, torque, and acceleration — delivering significant energy savings and dramatically reducing mechanical wear on pumps, fans, compressors, and conveyor drives. We engineer and assemble stand-alone VFD enclosures and multi-drive panels using drives from Allen-Bradley PowerFlex, Danfoss, ABB, Yaskawa, and Siemens SINAMICS platforms.

Every VFD panel includes input line reactors or harmonic filters where required to protect the drive and upstream power quality. We size input fusing, disconnect means, and output wiring correctly for both the drive and motor nameplate ratings, and program the drive parameters to match your specific application — whether that's a simple hand-off-auto pump station or a complex coordinated multi-drive system with ramp profiles and PID feedback.

Key Features & Capabilities

Allen-Bradley, ABB, Danfoss, Yaskawa, Siemens drive brands
Single drive and multi-drive enclosures (up to 10+ drives)
Input line reactors and harmonic filters for power quality
Drive parameter programming for your exact application
Hand-Off-Auto (HOA) selector switches and local speed pot
4–20mA / 0–10V analog speed reference inputs
Ethernet/IP and Modbus RTU communications to PLC/SCADA

Power Distribution Panels

High-capacity distribution centers for industrial and commercial facilities.

What We Build

Power distribution panels (PDPs) and main distribution boards (MDBs) are the nerve center of your facility's electrical system. We design and build custom distribution panels that take power from your utility or generator feed and distribute it cleanly and safely to downstream branch circuit panels, motor control centers, and individual equipment loads throughout your plant or building.

Our distribution panels are engineered with properly sized main circuit breakers or main lugs, copper bus bars rated for your full-load and fault current requirements, and individual branch circuit overcurrent protection devices (MCCBs or fuses) for every outgoing circuit. We integrate digital power meters with Modbus communication, surge protective devices (SPDs), and automatic transfer switches (ATS) when generator backup is required.

UL 508A Listed Panels

Certified industrial control panels meeting all North American safety standards.

What UL 508A Means

UL 508A is the Standard for Industrial Control Panels, published by Underwriters Laboratories (UL). A UL 508A Listed panel has been designed and built according to strict safety requirements covering component spacing, wire sizing, overcurrent protection, short-circuit current ratings (SCCR), grounding, and enclosure integrity. The listing mark tells your customer, insurer, and local authority having jurisdiction (AHJ) that the panel was independently evaluated and meets recognized safety standards.

Many OEMs, facility managers, and project owners require UL 508A certification before a control panel can be installed and energized. Without the listing, panels may be rejected by inspectors or require costly field evaluations. Our UL 508A Listed shop produces compliant panels efficiently — with full documentation, labeling, and the recognized mark applied at our facility, so there are no delays at your job site.

Our UL 508A Process

SCCR properly calculated and labeled on every panel
Component spacing per UL 508A Section 10 requirements
Wire sizing and ampacity per NEC Table 310 and UL rules
UL-Listed components used throughout — no substitutions
Grounding and bonding verified per UL and NEC requirements
Field labels and UL mark applied at our Listed shop
Full wiring diagrams and as-built documentation provided
Inspector-ready panel with marking, labeling, and nameplate
